## Assessment Overview

Your aim is to develop an **e-business (website or mobile application)** from **start-up to harvest** with features of **innovation, planning, and implementation** reflective of a true entrepreneurial small business. Ultimately, you will **research the opportunity, review its full potential, and convert your findings into a software application.**

The Group Project Assignment is broken down into two parts:

- **Part A: Business Proposal [Report & Pitch] — 30%**
- **Part B: Project Implementation & Demonstration — 30%**

**Groups:** 2 students per group. You work together for **both** parts, and **all group members receive the same mark.**

### Summary table

| Part | Brief detail | Length | Weighting | Due |
|------|--------------|--------|-----------|-----|
| **Part A: Business Proposal [Report & Pitch]** | **Report:** prepare a detailed report (see criteria/rubric). **Pitch:** record a three-minute video presenting a pitch for your business that can be used to convince investors to put money into your business. | Report: **500–1500 words**; Pitch: **3 minutes** | **30%** | **Week 2 (Session 5)** |
| **Part B: Project Implementation & Demonstration** | **Implementation:** develop an e-business website or mobile application (**mobile app = 10 bonus marks**). Your e-business site/app must display specific criteria and disclaimer information (detailed below). | Demonstration: **10 minutes**; Q&A: **5 minutes** | **30%** | **Week 4 (Session 10) [Implementation Code Files]** and **Session 11 [Demonstration]** |

### Learning Outcomes Assessed
- **LO 1:** Distinguish the various forms of ownership of small businesses, including IT businesses.
- **LO 2:** Evaluate various IT business opportunities.
- **LO 3:** Compose a proposal for starting and running a business.
- **LO 4:** Evaluate sources of finance for starting and running the business.

# PART A — Business Proposal [Report & Pitch] (30%)

## A.1 — Report (500–1500 words)

Your report should include **all** of the following:

**1. Executive Summary**
- a. Company information
- b. Market Opportunity
- c. Financial data

**2. Environment and Industry Analysis**
- a. Target customers
- b. Competitors
- c. Market forces
- d. Product outlook and potential

**3. Products or Services**
- a. Does the solution satisfy the requirements of the target market?
- b. Describe the look, feel, features, benefits — what makes it unique?

**4. Compliance**
- a. Registrations, licencing, taxation and other legal requirements

**5. Marketing, Research and Evaluation**
- a. Target market
- b. Branding
- c. Unique selling proposition (USP)
- d. Look, feel, features
- e. Advertising and promotion
- f. Additional features:
  - a. Social media link
  - b. Location map/s
  - c. Attractive graphics

**6. Manufacturing & Operations Plan, Management Team, Timeline, Exit Strategy**
- a. Management Team
- b. M&O Framework
- c. System integration testing & data collection
- d. Proposed timeline
- e. Overview of the Exit Strategy

**7. Financial Plan**
- a. Income statement
- b. Cash flow projection
- c. Balance Sheet

## A.2 — Pitch (3-minute Panopto video)

You will record a **three-minute Panopto video** presenting a pitch for your business that can be used to **convince investors** to put money into your business.

Your 3-minute pitch should overview **all** of the following:

**1. Key Points**
- a. Problem Statement
- b. Solution
- c. Market size
- d. Competitors
- e. Unique value of your solution
- f. Who are your team?
- g. Financial Summary
- h. What do you want?

**2. Basic slides** (not more than 5)

**3. Record a video of the Pitch using Panopto**
- a. How to record a video using Panopto for Mac
- b. How to record a video using Panopto for Windows
- c. How to Submit your Panopto video

## A.3 — Rubric: Part A (Business Proposal)

Grade bands per criterion: **High Distinction / Distinction / Credit / Pass / Not yet Satisfactory.**

### Executive Summary — [16–20 / 14–15 / 12–13 / 10–11 / 0–9 points]
- **High Distinction (16–20):** Executive Summary is **clear, concise, and generates excitement.** All key information is introduced: Company information, Market Opportunity, Financial data.
- **Distinction (14–15):** Executive Summary is clear and generates excitement. All key information introduced.
- **Credit (12–13):** Executive Summary is clear and introduces most of the key information.
- **Pass (10–11):** Executive Summary includes some key information, but is unclear.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–9):** Executive Summary is missing or inconsistent with the information provided.

### Environment & Industry Analysis — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 1–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** Analysis is **critical, objective, and well researched.** Identifies and describes: target customers, competitors, market forces, and product outlook/potential.
- **Distinction (7):** A well-researched and clearly articulated analysis. All key elements identified and described.
- **Credit (6):** Analysis is clear and includes evidence of research. Most key elements identified and described.
- **Pass (5):** Analysis is mostly speculative. Some key elements are missing.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (1–4):** Analysis is missing or incomplete.

### Products or Services — [16–20 / 14–15 / 12–13 / 10–11 / 0–9 points]
- **HD (16–20):** Product or Service solution satisfies target market requirements. The solution description highlights: look, feel, features, benefits, what makes it unique.
- **Distinction (14–15):** Solution considers most target-market requirements. All key points covered in the solution description.
- **Credit (12–13):** Solution considers some target-market requirements. The solution description covers most key points.
- **Pass (10–11):** Solution is general. Consideration of the target market is not evident. Key selling points are missing.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–9):** Little to no evidence of effort made to consider product/service strengths or to present the product/service in a favourable light.

### Compliance — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** All compliance requirements identified and addressed, including: Registrations, Licences, Taxation, Legal requirements.
- **Distinction (7):** Most compliance requirements have been identified and addressed.
- **Credit (6):** Key compliance requirements identified and addressed, but some detail is missing.
- **Pass (5):** Compliance requirements identified, but significant detail is missing.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** Compliance not considered, or poorly understood.

### Marketing, Research & Evaluation — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** **Strong evidence** of research and evaluation demonstrated. Marketing strategy considers: Target market, Branding, Unique selling proposition, Look/feel and features, Advertising and promotion. The following additional features are included: social media link, location map/s, attractive graphics.
- **Distinction (7):** Strong evidence of research and evaluation demonstrated. Marketing strategy considers: Target market, Branding, Unique selling proposition, Look/feel and features, Advertising and promotion.
- **Credit (6):** Evidence of research and evaluation demonstrated. Marketing strategy considers most key factors.
- **Pass (5):** Some evidence of research and evaluation demonstrated. Marketing strategy considers some key factors.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** Insufficient evidence of marketing, research and evaluation.

### Manufacturing & Operations Plan / Management Team / Timeline / Exit Strategy — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** M&O Plan is **clear and concise, and considers implementation challenges.** Includes all of the following: Management team overview, Definition of the M&O Framework, Plans for System integration testing and data collection, Proposed timeline, A concise overview of the Exit Strategy.
- **Distinction (7):** M&O Plan is clear and concise, with some consideration for implementation challenges. Includes all key elements.
- **Credit (6):** M&O Plan is clear and detailed. Most of the key elements are included.
- **Pass (5):** M&O Plan includes most of the key elements, but lacks clarity or misses important detail.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** The M&O Plan does not adequately address the requirements.

### Financial Plan — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** Financial plan is **clear and detailed, and points to a viable business solution.** Includes complete and realistic overviews of: Income statement, Cash flow projection, Balance sheet.
- **Distinction (7):** Financial plan is detailed and points to a viable business solution. Overviews of all financial statements provided.
- **Credit (6):** Financial plan is detailed, but viability of the business solution is questionable. Overviews of all financial statements provided.
- **Pass (5):** Overviews of all financial statements provided; however, the Financial plan is unclear.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** The Financial Plan is incomplete or not considered.

### Pitch — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** Pitch is **succinct, well presented, and compelling, balancing business and emotional needs.** Excellent time-management (3 minutes). Pitch includes: Problem statement, Solution, Market size, Competitors, Unique value of your solution, Team, Financial summary, What do you want?
- **Distinction (7):** Pitch is well presented and compelling, balancing business and emotional needs. Good time management (approximately 3 minutes).
- **Credit (6):** A well-presented pitch that addresses business needs, but overlooks emotional needs. Pitch timing is too long or too short.
- **Pass (5):** Pitch misses key information and/or does little to persuade or excite the audience. Poor time management.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** Pitch not delivered.

### Part A — Overall Score bands
- **High Distinction:** 80% and above
- **Distinction:** 70% – 79%
- **Credit:** 60% – 69%
- **Pass:** 50% – 59%
- **Not Satisfactory:** below 50%

# PART B — Project Implementation & Demonstration (30%)

You will start by developing **either an e-business website or a mobile application** (a **mobile app attracts 10 bonus marks**).

## B.1 — Implementation requirements

**1. Look and Feel should be:**
- a. Functional with a well-designed layout
- b. Aesthetically pleasing
- c. Intuitive — is it easy to read and understand?
- d. Simple — is it memorable, meaningful?

**2. Design and Implementation**
- a. Should align to:
  - a. Problem statement
  - b. Customer profile
  - c. Core Value Proposition
- b. Does it work? Does information flow?

**3. Innovative Features should consider:**
- a. Do they address the problem?
- b. Does the product create or improve a service?
- c. Does the product consolidate functions into one tool?
- d. Does it reduce user effort, save money or time?

**4. Product or Service completeness** — the criteria below **must be displayed** on your website or mobile app:
- a. **ABN/ACN number (temporary)**
- b. **Contact Us page**
- c. **Online Payment method** (e.g. PayPal)
- d. **Marketing and promotion** of your products
- e. **List of available services**
- f. Additional points will be awarded for:
  - a. Link to Social Media
  - b. Location Maps
  - c. Attractive graphics

**5. Disclaimer information** on your website/app: **"This website/app is for a class assignment and not for commercial purposes"**

## B.2 — Demonstration

**1. Presentation/demo should:**
- a. Cover all key requirements
- b. Highlight key features
- c. Engage the audience
- d. Reflect teamwork and collaboration

**2. Outsourcing:** If you need to outsource your coding, you **must include all communication logs** with your submission.

## B.3 — Rubric: Part B (Implementation)

Grade bands per criterion: **High Distinction / Distinction / Credit / Pass / Not yet Satisfactory.**

### Look and Feel — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** Excellent use of user-centered design principles: **Functionality** (well-designed layout), **Aesthetics** (alluring without being overwhelming), **Intuition** (easy to read and understand), **Simplicity** (memorable and meaningful).
- **Distinction (7):** Good use of user-centered design principles. Stronger in some areas relative to others (Functionality, Aesthetics, Intuition, Simplicity).
- **Credit (6):** Good use of user-centered design principles, but some areas are missing.
- **Pass (5):** Some evidence of use of user-centered design principles, but more effort is required.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** Little to no evidence that user-centered design principles considered or applied.

### Design and Implementation — [32–40 / 28–31 / 24–27 / 20–23 / 0–19 points]
- **HD (32–40):** Solution design is **well-aligned** to the: Problem statement, Customer profile, Core Value Proposition. **Implementation is successful.**
- **Distinction (28–31):** Solution design well aligned to business requirements. Implementation is generally successful, but minor bugs or information flow impediments are evident.
- **Credit (24–27):** Solution design addresses some business requirements. Implementation is generally successful, but minor bugs or information flow impediments are evident.
- **Pass (20–23):** Solution design addresses few of the business requirements. Implementation reveals major bugs or information flow impediments.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–19):** Design and implementation not considered.

### Innovative Features — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** Features are **simple, original and creative**, and address areas including: Address the problem or need, Create or improve a service, Consolidate multiple functions into one tool, Reduce user effort, Save money and/or time.
- **Distinction (7):** Features are simple and creative, and address a broad range of areas.
- **Credit (6):** Solution has one or more features that are simple and creative, and address a range of areas.
- **Pass (5):** Features are simple and creative, but do not address areas in a constructive way.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** Solution has no innovative features.

### Product or Service Completeness — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** All business aspects considered during development: Functional area description, A.B.N. displayed, Contact us page, Maps. **All transactions functioning correctly:** Single payment, Bill generation.
- **Distinction (7):** All key business aspects considered during development. Most transactions are functioning correctly.
- **Credit (6):** Key business aspects considered during development, but some are missing. Most transactions are functioning correctly.
- **Pass (5):** Some business aspects considered during development, but many are missing. Transactions have not been adequately tested/implemented.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** The product or service is incomplete.

### Presentation/Demonstration — [8–10 / 7 / 6 / 5 / 0–4 points]
- **HD (8–10):** Presentation covers **all key requirements** in a timely, concise, and well-organised manner. Product/Service demo is engaging and highlights key features. Effective, collaborative teamwork is evident, **with all team members contributing equally.**
- **Distinction (7):** Presentation covers all key points, and is timely, clear and well organised. Product/Service demo is engaging and highlights key features. Effective, collaborative teamwork is evident.
- **Credit (6):** Presentation covers most key points and is clear, but not well timed. Product/Service demo is engaging and highlights key features. Some evidence of collaborative teamwork.
- **Pass (5):** Presentation covers some key points, but runs over time or under time. Product/Service is described at a basic level. Little evidence of effective teamwork.
- **Not yet Satisfactory (0–4):** Limited evidence of effort to address presentation/demonstration criteria.
